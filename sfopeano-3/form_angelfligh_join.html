Volunteer Pilots Needed

If you love to fly, have access to an airplane and can take an occasional day off to make a difference in a child or adult's life, Angel Flight Mid-Atlantic has an opportunity for you! Angel Flight Mid-Atlantic matches passengers with pilots who generously donate their time, aircraft and fuel at no cost.
Angel Flight Mid-Atlantic pilots give because they care and want to help those who have a genuine financial and urgent need. Volunteer pilots are matched with missions originating in or linking through the District of Columbia, Delaware, Kentucky, Maryland, Michigan, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Virginia and West Virginia.

Pre-qualification

1. Do you live or fly in the District of Columbia, Delaware, Kentucky, Maryland, Michigan, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Virginia and West Virginia?

Angel Flight Mid-Atlantic serves District of Columbia, Delaware, Kentucky, Maryland, Michigan, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Virginia and West Virginia. If you live or fly in these areas, we encourage you to join. For opportunities in other areas visit Air Care Alliance

2. Are you a pilot?

We require a minimum of 500 hours as PIC in order to become a Command Pilot. However, we encourage you to join since there are many volunteer opportunites for you while you build your hours.
We encourage you to join even as a student pilot since there are many volunteer opportunites for you while you build your hours.
One of the best ways to help us is to join Angel Flight Mid-Atlantic as a non-pilot member. There are a number of ways you can contribute other than flying. We encourage non-pilots to join.

Do you own, rent or otherwise have access to an airplane?

Many of our Command Pilots rent aircraft, or have access to an aircraft through a friend.
